# Gerer Events — Business Plan & Monetization Strategy (v1)

## 1) Executive Summary
Gerer Events is a premium event platform focused on the full event lifecycle:
- Before event: luxury invitation + RSVP
- During event: QR check-in reliability
- After event: guest media collection and insights

Market entry starts in Cameroon with **XAF-first pricing** and strong visual differentiation via Afro-luxury design language.

---

## 2) Core Market Problem
Most existing event tools are either:
- Utility-first and visually bland
- Too complex for one-time hosts
- Weak on post-event engagement

Users need a product that is:
1. Beautiful enough to impress guests
2. Operationally reliable on event day
3. Useful after the event finishes

---

## 3) Positioning Strategy
**Primary Positioning:** “The most beautiful way to invite people to real-world events.”

Differentiation pillars:
- Premium editorial visuals (Afro-luxury identity)

## 5) Revenue Model (Hybrid)
Use both one-time and recurring pricing to capture different user behaviors.

## Pricing (Launch, XAF)
- **Free:** 0 XAF
  - 1 event
  - Basic template
  - Watermark
  - Limited capacity

- **Event Pass (One-Time): 15,000 XAF / event**
  - Premium template
  - No watermark
  - Higher RSVP/media limits

- **Studio Monthly: 25,000 XAF / month**
  - Multiple active events
  - Better analytics
  - Exports
  - Suitable for recurring creators/planners

- **Agency: 75,000 XAF / month**
  - Team seats
  - White-label controls
  - Priority support

---

## 6) Monetization Ladder
1. Acquire with Free tier
2. Convert with Event Pass (single event need)
3. Retain with Studio Monthly
4. Expand ARPU via Agency and add-ons

Add-on opportunities:
- Premium template packs
- Reminder credits (SMS/WhatsApp)
- Custom event domain
- AI post-event highlight generation
- Printable QR/signage assets

## 9) Unit Logic & Margin Considerations
Primary costs:
- Hosting and database
- Media storage and transfer
- Notification delivery
- Payment processing fees

Margin strategy:
- Keep Free tier constrained to force meaningful upgrade triggers
- Use premium design as core conversion lever
- Push repeat users to monthly plans
- Prioritize agency clients for stable recurring revenue

---

## 10) Risk Analysis + Mitigation
## Risk 1: Users compare with free alternatives
Mitigation:
- Design quality and cultural identity as non-commodity value
- Better wedding-specific emotional experience

## Risk 2: Event-day operational failure fear
Mitigation:
- High-speed QR scanner UX
- Fallback check-in options and robust data sync

## Risk 3: Slow paid conversion
Mitigation:
- Strong premium template previews
- Time-limited seasonal offers
- Clear limits on Free plan

## Risk 4: Growth stalls after social events
Mitigation:
- Expand to planners and then corporate segment
- Build B2B-friendly workflows early
